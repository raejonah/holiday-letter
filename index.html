Happy holidays, from my home to yours!

2025 was a year of big achievements and big changes. In June, I completed my Ph.D. in Physics and Astronomy at UC Irvine, where my research focused on understanding exoplanets and the stars they orbit. I successfully defended my thesis, defeated the snake , and celebrated a job well done.

Sadly, my grandfather Terry Holcomb (the first "Dr. Holcomb") passed away towards the end of 2024. In January 2025, the Holcomb family came together to celebrate his life. It was a joyous occasion, as he would have wanted. Terry and Rita (who passed in 2023) are memorialized as "silent teachers" in the UCI Willed Body Program Memorial.

In September I moved to College Park, Maryland, to begin a postdoctoral fellowship at NASA Goddard Space Flight Center. My work here is on the Pandora SmallSat (a very small mission!) and the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope (a very big mission!) Both telescopes will be launching in 2026, and I can't wait to see what they reveal to us about the universe!

Living near DC has been quite an adventure. My work was heavily affected by the government shutdown in October and November, but I'm still employed and have had the chance to engage in public outreach and scientific advocacy on Capitol Hill. I've also been having fun setting up my new apartment , making new friends , and exploring the city (there are so many farmers markets here!)

Between the bustle, it has been a great year for my hobby of creative writing. One of my short stories got selected for the Metastellar "Best of 2024" anthology , and I will have another story appearing in different literary magazine in summer 2026. I've had great fun continuing my work as the editor of the webcomic Foreach , and even got to attend a comic fair and sell merch for the first time.

This year was also full of excursions! Outside of school and work, I found plenty of time to get off the beaten path, whether that was roadtripping with Mom, to traveling to conferences, celebrating my 10-year high school reunion, dressing up for fantasy conventions and renn faires, attending oh ! so ! many ! weddings !

But like always, the best part of 2025 was the people. Whether that was reconnecting with old friends, making new ones, getting to know my coworkers, or spending time with my loved ones â€” it's all of you that make my life so special.

Here's to a fantastic 2026!

Rae Holcomb
